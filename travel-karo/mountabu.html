Mount Abu


Mount Abu is a popular hill station in the Aravalli Range in Sirohi district of Rajasthan state in western India, near the border with Gujarat. The mountain forms a distinct rocky plateau 22 km long by 9 km wide. The highest peak on the mountain is Guru Shikhar at 1,722 m (5,650 ft) above sea level. It is referred to as 'an oasis in the desert' as its heights are home to rivers, lakes, waterfalls and evergreen forests. The nearest train station is Abu Road railway station: 28 km away.

Local Food and Festivals

Royalty is in the very air you breathe in Mount Abu. And it is palpable in the aromas that waft from its kitchens. The food in Mount Abu, just like the rest of Rajasthan, is a royal treat for both vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike. The traditional Rajasthani Thali depicts a brilliant play of gourmet preparations on a plate of epic proportions. The meticulous arrangements of curries, pickles, special breads and desserts is sure to delight one and all. If you don’t have the appetite for one, you could go à la carte. The traditional Daal Baati (roasted wheat balls in lentil gravy) and Gatte ki Subzi (gram flour dumpling curry) figure right on top of the list of must-try dishes. Do stop by one of the many roadside stalls for the typical Pohe they serve. There are also plenty of options for non-vegetarians too, like the famous Safed Maas (white meat) or Laal Maas (red meat), both tantalising curries that are cooked over slow fire. Dil Jaani is a dessert you just cannot miss! Boondi ke ladoo, a sweet treat with cashews, raisins, saffron and the light flavor of cardamom, is a famous Indian dessert usually made during festive times like Diwali.
