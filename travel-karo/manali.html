Manali


Manali is a resort town nestled in the mountains of the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh near the northern end of the Kullu Valley in the Beas River Valley. It is located in the Kullu district, about 270 km (168 mi) north of the state capital, Shimla, 309 km (192 miles) northeast of Chandigarh and 544 km (338 miles) northeast of Delhi, the federal capital. The small town, with a population of 8,096, is the beginning of an ancient trade route to Ladakh and from there over the Karakoram Pass on to Yarkand and Khotan in the Tarim Basin. It is a popular tourist destination and serves as the gateway to Lahaul and Spiti district as well as Leh.

Local Food and Festivals

Mountain food, though simple, is rich and tasty. The things that go into making your food mostly comes from someone’s backyard in the village itself and hence is unbelievably fresh and lip smacking. A refreshing variety of vegetable dishes alongside a great selection of meat dishes is what’s waiting for you in Manali. The thick, milky, delicious cup of tea is relished throughout the day. Drink a tall glass of lassi to refresh yourself after a tiring day or add a huge dollop of ghee on that roti before you dunk it in that delicious curry. Try the typical mountain cuisine of Manali which includes rice, madra (a Himachali specialty of yogurt based gravy often with chickpeas), kadhi (gravy of beaten curd), raita (made with dry fruits with a liberal addition of mustard), a variety of pulses and lentils. You will start swearing by this food! Non vegetarians must try the local fish curry, of river trout caught in the rivers of Manali and cooked with flavourful local spices. Generally eaten with rice, this fish curry is a culinary marriage of tangy and spicy flavors.
