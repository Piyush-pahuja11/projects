Local Food and Festivals

Fish, rice and feni – are arguably the three most important things to a Goan. Being a coastal land, it is not surprising that rice and fish curry are the Goa’s famous food. But a wide variety of fish that are available means an array of fish curries using a range of ingredients, especially flavourful spices. There are many other culinary delights like Vindaloo, Sorpotel, or chicken Xacuti - one of the most wonderful coastal (Goan and Konkan) preparations of chicken. There’s also traditional Saraswat food, all of which are an essential part of Goan cuisine. The lobster Peri Peri is a ‘Must-Try’ here. Portuguese influence is evident in the Goan culture and also in the style of cooking. The lobster is covered in hot chilli marinade and cooked to perfection. Food in Goa is simply irresistible.

But Goa is not only about food but also the festivals. They are celebrated with great fervor. Cultural demonstrations, dancing and singing are a major part of the various famous local festivals of Goa.The Goan Carnival is one of the most exciting celebrations in Goa. Celebrated just before Lent, there are colourful parades with bands playing feet tapping Goan music, dances and floats out all night on the streets. It’s a time for feasting, drinking and merry-making for all the Goans. The Lairai Zatra is another popular festival which resembles more of a fair. It is held to revere Goddess Lairai and is commemorated by fire walking at the Shirgao Temple. Also, Mahashivaratri is a big event here. Dedicated to Lord Manguesh, the temple in Ponda taluk and is sacred to Hindus.
